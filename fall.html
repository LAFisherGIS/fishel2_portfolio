Fall Quarter, 2021

First Steps in the Master's Program

Fall Quarter was spent studying two main fields, those being the contemporary discourse in regarding the tech field and the dangers of the ways data is commodified by it, and the fundamentals of web mapping as a discipline. In addition to those things, considerable time was also spent working on the preliminary form of a project proposal for my capstone for the MSGT, but this is not the page for it.

To see an example of my web mapping coursework, simply click any one of the links in the Lab Pages sidebar on the right. Lab 1 contained no actual cartographic component, and so has been excluded from this portfolio. These labs constitute the full breadth of my graded web mapping experience as of December 2021. Note that because sidebars have been omitted from the lab pages to emphasize the actual lab content, the links will open into new tabs to avoid the need to manually reopen this page to access further labs.

Personal Philosophy

Every ton of carbon we release into the atmosphere and every kilogram of metal we refine then dump into a landfill during our lifetimes represents something taken from the future for our own benefit. We, as those currently alive, have an affirmative duty to those who come after us to restrain these takings to only that which is to sustainable, and to give back as much as we take in whatever way achievable. Doing otherwise is akin to mortgaging our collective future as a species with no repayment plan, in the name of immediate gratification and expedience.
